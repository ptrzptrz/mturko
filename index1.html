How ELO Rating Works

Your rating is calculated using the Glicko-2 system, an advanced algorithm used by FIDE and major chess platforms. It considers not just wins and losses, but the strength of your opponents.

Each player has three hidden values: Rating (your skill estimate), Rating Deviation (RD, how certain the system is), and Volatility (how consistently you perform).

When you're new, your RD is high — meaning the system is uncertain about your level. Your rating will change significantly after each game. As you play more, RD decreases and your rating stabilizes.

Beating a higher-rated player gives more points than beating a lower-rated one. Drawing with a much stronger player is rewarded, while drawing with a much weaker one costs points.
